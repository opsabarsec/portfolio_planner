# Freelance AI Market Intelligence Module

## Purpose
This module guides research agents in searching for current trends, opportunities, and skill demand in the freelance AI engineering market. Use this when researching portfolio project ideas, in-demand AI skills, freelance rates, certifications, or what clients are hiring for.

## Primary Sources

### Job Boards & Platforms
- **Upwork** - Filter: AI/ML, 2025-2026 postings, sort by recent. Look for: job titles, rates, required skills, project duration, client reviews of AI engineer work.
- **Malt** (European-focused) - AI/data science category, filter France/EU. Note: rates are typically higher in Europe.
- **Toptal** - Premium freelance talent pool. Check blog for trend reports and salary surveys.
- **LinkedIn** (Jobs & Trending) - Search "AI engineer freelance", "remote AI", "agent developer". Track skill endorsements, hiring manager commentary, salary insights.
- **Hacker News "Who's Hiring"** - Monthly threads. Filter by "freelance", "contract", "remote AI". Look for: required skills, tech stack mentions, rates implied.
- **Y Combinator Job Board** - Filter: AI/ML, remote, freelance/contract. Higher-quality startups looking for specialized AI work.
- **freelance.de / Guru / PeoplePerHour** - Alternative platforms, often have different client base than Upwork.

### Community & Trend Sources
- **Dev.to** - Articles on "freelance AI engineer", "portfolio projects that get hired", "AI certifications worth it"
- **Reddit** (r/freelance, r/MachineLearning, r/OpenAI) - Discussions about rates, market saturation, what projects clients want
- **Substack & AI Newsletters** (DAIR.AI, The Batch, Hugging Face Weekly) - Market commentary, emerging skill demand
- **X/Twitter** - Follow AI thought leaders discussing freelance opportunities, MCP adoption trends, agentic AI market
- **GitHub Trending** - What open-source AI projects are getting stars (proxy for market interest)

## Search Strategy

### Query Types & Examples

**Market Trends (Current Rates & Demand)**
- "AI engineer freelance rates 2025"
- "most hired AI skills freelance 2025"
- "MCP server development freelance demand"
- "agentic RAG consultant market 2025"
- "Upwork AI engineer earnings 2025"
- "LLM applications freelance opportunities"

**Portfolio Project Ideas**
- "AI engineer portfolio projects 2025 that get clients"
- "agentic RAG portfolio ideas freelance"
- "MCP server portfolio project example"
- "B2B AI automation portfolio projects"
- "AI portfolio projects trending 2025"
- "what portfolio projects do clients hire for"

**Certifications & Credentials**
- "AI certifications worth it freelance 2025"
- "Google AI certificate freelance value"
- "AWS AI practitioner certification freelance demand"
- "LLM engineering credentials recognized by clients"
- "MCP certification available 2025"
- "agent engineering credentials 2025-2026"

**Skills & Technologies**
- "in-demand AI skills freelance 2025"
- "FastAPI RAG projects 2025 freelance"
- "Docker multi-agent systems freelance"
- "MongoDB vector database freelance demand"
- "Claude API vs OpenAI API freelance preference"

### Search Approach

1. **Recency Filter**: Search ONLY for sources published or updated in 2024 or 2025. Exclude anything dated 2023 or earlier unless it's a fundamental resource (e.g., foundational MCP docs). Always verify publication date explicitly.

2. **Multiple Query Angles**: Don't rely on single query. For each topic, use:
   - Direct phrase: "AI freelance market trends 2025"
   - Platform-specific: "Upwork AI engineer 2025"
   - Community voice: "reddit AI freelance 2025"
   - Expert perspective: "X LinkedIn AI freelance thoughts 2025"

3. **Client Voice First**: Prioritize sources where clients describe what they're hiring for (job postings, Upwork descriptions, HN Who's Hiring) over purely educational content.

4. **Quantitative Data**: Look for:
   - Hourly/project rates with dates
   - Number of open positions by skill
   - Job posting volume trends
   - Salary surveys (e.g., Toptal's annual salary report)

5. **Stack-Specific Signals**: When evaluating portfolio project ideas, check if they emphasize:
   - Python backend (FastAPI, Django)
   - Docker containerization
   - Vector databases / RAG
   - Multi-agent systems
   - MCP integration
   - LLM orchestration

## Coverage Validation

After completing research, confirm you've covered:
- [ ] At least 2 freelance job board sources (Upwork, Malt, Toptal, etc.)
- [ ] At least 1 community source (Reddit, HN, Dev.to, X)
- [ ] At least 1 trend/insight source (newsletter, blog, YouTube)
- [ ] For portfolio ideas: at least 3 concrete project examples with client demand signals
- [ ] For certifications: at least 3 options with freelance value assessment
- [ ] For skills: ranking of top 5 in-demand technologies with 2025-2026 context

## Output Format

When reporting findings, organize by:
1. **Current Market State** - What are clients paying for right now?
2. **Skill Demand Ranking** - Top 5 most-hired AI skills for freelance work
3. **Portfolio Recommendations** - 3 project ideas with rationale
4. **Certifications & Credentials** - Options ranked by freelance signal strength
5. **Emerging Opportunities** - Skills/areas with growing demand but less saturation
6. **Quick Wins** - Skills to highlight immediately without waiting for certs

All findings must include source citation with publication date (YYYY-MM-DD where available).
