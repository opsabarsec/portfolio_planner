# EU AI Jobs and Skills Module

## Purpose
Use this module to identify which AI skills and roles show strongest hiring demand in Europe, and translate that hiring demand into portfolio opportunities that align with recruiter expectations and market movement.

## Goal
Translate hiring demand signals into concrete portfolio opportunities for the European market. Focus on which technical capabilities and project types are most visible in job postings, what makes portfolios competitive, and which skills map to sustainable European employment trends.

## Search Focus

### Primary geographies
- France, Germany, Netherlands, Belgium, Switzerland, UK
- Remote EU-based roles
- Generalist Europe hiring data

### Skill and role categories
- AI engineer jobs Europe
- LLM engineer jobs France Germany Netherlands
- Agentic AI jobs Europe
- MLOps and AI platform roles
- RAG implementation roles
- AI evaluation and observability roles
- Governance and compliance AI roles
- AI infrastructure and inference roles

## Extraction Targets

When searching, extract:
- Repeated stack keywords across multiple job postings
- Architecture patterns (monolithic vs. microservices, inference strategies, RAG patterns)
- Compliance or language requirements (GDPR, multilingual, data residency)
- Cloud and deployment expectations (AWS, Azure, GCP, on-prem, edge)
- Industries hiring most actively (financial services, healthcare, manufacturing, public sector)
- Required experience levels and typical career paths
- Salary and compensation signals (where disclosed)
- Hiring urgency signals (actively recruiting, growth teams)

### Search Approach

1. **Recency Filter**: Prioritize job postings and salary data from 2026. Exclude evergreen career guides unless they have 2026 data updates.

2. **Multiple Angles**: Don't rely on single source. Use:
   - Job boards: LinkedIn Jobs, Indeed Europe, Stack Overflow Jobs, specialized boards (MLjobs.eu, etc.)
   - Salary data: Levels.fyi, Salaryexplorer, Glassdoor Europe, Toptal reports
   - Community voice: Reddit r/learnprogramming Europe, Hacker News Who's Hiring
   - Company pages: Direct hiring from major European AI labs and startups

3. **Hiring Manager Voice First**: Prioritize sources where recruiters/hiring managers describe what they're looking for (job postings, recruiter blogs, hiring announcements) over general career advice.

4. **Stack Extraction**: When analyzing jobs, note:
   - Backend frameworks (FastAPI, Django, Go)
   - LLM libraries (LangChain, LlamaIndex, Claude SDK, custom)
   - Vector databases (Pinecone, Weaviate, Chroma, proprietary)
   - Deployment platforms (Docker, Kubernetes, cloud services)
   - Observability/eval tools (Weights & Biases, etc.)
   - Data and compliance tools (encryption, audit logging, GDPR tools)

5. **Compensation Context**: Extract salary ranges, growth potential, and equity signals to assess market maturity and investment level.

## Coverage Validation

After completing research, confirm you've covered:
- [ ] At least 3 job boards or sources (LinkedIn Jobs, Indeed, specialized AI job boards)
- [ ] At least 2 geographic regions within Europe (e.g., France + Germany, or Netherlands + UK)
- [ ] At least 2 sectors (e.g., fintech + manufacturing, or healthcare + public sector)
- [ ] At least 5 concrete job postings analyzed for stack and skills
- [ ] Current salary/compensation ranges with 2026 dates
- [ ] Repeated skill and tech stack patterns across 3+ postings
- [ ] Portfolio and credential signals from hiring manager feedback

## Output Format

When reporting hiring demand findings, organize by:

1. **Hiring Demand Summary** - Which AI roles and skills are hiring fastest in Europe right now?

2. **Skill Demand Ranking** - Top 5-7 most-hired AI skills with frequency, geography, and sector signals
   - Skill name
   - Hiring frequency (rare, moderate, hot)
   - Top 3 geographies hiring for this
   - Salary/compensation signal (if available)
   - Example job titles

3. **Tech Stack Patterns** - Most common architecture and tool patterns across job postings
   - Backend/framework
   - LLM integration approach
   - Data/RAG strategy
   - Deployment and observability
   - Example jobs emphasizing this stack

4. **Portfolio Opportunities from Hiring Signals** - 2-3 project ideas that directly address hiring demand
   - Why this addresses market hiring demand
   - Evidence from job postings
   - What technical capabilities it demonstrates
   - Which sectors/geographies value this most
   - Best audience (recruiter facing vs. peer credibility)

5. **Sector Movement** - Which industries are hiring fastest for AI, and what that signals
   - Sector
   - Hiring temperature
   - Skill priorities
   - Portfolio angle

6. **Career Path Signals** - How European AI roles are structured (growth paths, typical progressions, team composition)

All findings must include source citation with publication date (YYYY-MM-DD where available).
